<Section icon={Target} title="Early-Game Progression Steps">

The initial phase of your gym development focuses entirely on building a reliable financial foundation. During this stage, resources are scarce, meaning every upgrade must yield immediate returns. Avoid the temptation to buy expensive, specialized machines early on. Instead, focus on high-turnover equipment that caters to the widest demographic of virtual gym-goers.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Optimize the Reception Area">
    Upgrade your check-in counter and hire a reliable receptionist. This ensures that incoming members are processed quickly, reducing wait times and preventing early departures.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish the Cardio Zone">
    Install basic treadmills and stationary bikes. These machines have low maintenance costs and high appeal, making them perfect for generating steady, early-game income.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Introduce Free Weights">
    Add dumbbell racks and basic flat benches. Strength training equipment attracts a different class of members who stay longer and pay higher individual session fees.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Hire Maintenance Staff">
    Recruit at least one janitor to keep the equipment clean. Dirty machines break down faster and cause member satisfaction ratings to drop rapidly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once these four steps are complete, you will have a self-sustaining loop that generates enough profit to fund larger expansion projects. Monitor your daily reports to identify which machines are constantly occupied and which ones sit idle.

|Equipment|Cost Rating|Income Yield|Maintenance Need|
|---|---|---|---|
|Treadmill|Low|Moderate|Low|
|Exercise Bike|Low|Low|Very Low|
|Dumbbell Rack|Moderate|Moderate|Low|
|Bench Press|Moderate|High|Moderate|

<Callout type="warning" title="Warning">
Do not expand your floor space until you have fully upgraded your existing machines. Expanding too early increases your daily utility bills without guaranteeing a matching increase in revenue.
</Callout>

</Section>
