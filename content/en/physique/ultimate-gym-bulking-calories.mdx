<Section icon={Swords} title="Macronutrient Distribution: Protein, Carbs, and Fats">

Calorie quantity dictates whether you gain or lose weight, but macronutrient quality dictates whether that weight is muscle or fat. A classic bodybuilding macro split focuses on high protein intake to support heavy lifting, moderate-to-high carbohydrates for workout fuel, and essential fats for hormonal health.

Protein is the absolute cornerstone of this protocol. When pushing your body to its physical limits, aiming for up to 2 grams of protein per pound of body weight ensures your muscles remain fully saturated with amino acids throughout the day.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Bulking Meal Plan Setup">

Structuring your daily meals is just as important as hit-testing your total calories. Spacing your meals every 2 to 3 hours prevents your body from entering a catabolic state and ensures a steady stream of nutrients to your recovering muscle groups.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Breakfast Foundation">
    Start your day immediately with high-quality proteins and complex carbs. A combination of egg whites, whole eggs, and oatmeal provides both fast and slow-digesting nutrients to break the overnight fast.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Pre-Workout Fueling">
    Consume a meal rich in lean protein and easily digestible carbohydrates 90 minutes before training. Chicken breast and white rice are ideal as they digest quickly without causing stomach discomfort during heavy lifts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Intra-Workout Energy">
    For high-volume workouts lasting over 60 minutes, utilize simple carbs like rice cakes or specialized carbohydrate powders to maintain high blood glucose levels and maximize your pump.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Post-Workout Recovery">
    Rehydrate and refuel immediately after training with a fast-digesting protein shake and simple carbs. Follow this up with a whole food meal like a rich beef or chicken bowl within 2 hours.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Pre-Bed Hormonal Support">
    Before sleeping, focus on slow-digesting proteins like micellar casein or whole food fats to keep your muscles fed overnight. Taper your carbohydrate intake during this final meal to prioritize recovery.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Liquid Calories are Your Friend">
If you struggle to hit your target calories through solid food alone, incorporate liquid meals. A homemade shake with whole milk, oats, peanut butter, and whey protein can easily deliver 1,000 clean calories without leaving you feeling overly bloated.
</Callout>

</Section>
