<Section icon={Target} title="Optimizing Power Spots and Max Monday Events">

Power Spots represent a newer frontier for those mastering **ultimate gym controls**. Unlike traditional gyms, Power Spots are tied to the Dynamax system and have a lower placement limit of 10. These spots are particularly valuable during "Max Monday" events, where specific species like Caterpie or Beldum might appear with increased frequency. The goal here is often not gold, but the acquisition of Max Particles and specific species candy.

| Feature | Gym Mechanics | Power Spot Mechanics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Max Capacity** | 20 Units | 10 Units |
| **Turnover Rate** | Player-driven combat | Timer-based expiration |
| **Feeding** | Berries for Motivation | N/A (Particle based) |
| **Rewards** | PokéCoins | Candy / Max Particles |
| **Interaction** | Battle / Raid | Dynamax Battle |

The strategy for Power Spots involves identifying which Pokémon are currently "candy-locked." Species like Duraludon and Kubfu are notoriously difficult to power up through walking alone. By placing them in Power Spots, you create a passive income of candy that is essential for reaching Level 50.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Hearts and Buddy Progression">

The final piece of the **ultimate gym controls** puzzle is the integration of the Buddy System. Every time you take over a gym, you have an opportunity to earn a "Battle Heart" for your current buddy. By rotating your buddy into your battle party before clearing a gym, you can significantly speed up the progress toward "Best Buddy" status.

**Buddy Battle Strategies:**
- **The Swap Method**: Before the final blow to a gym defender, swap your buddy into the active slot.
- **The Party Lock**: Keep your current buddy in a pre-set "Gym Clearer" battle party.
- **Multi-Buddy Rotation**: If you take 20 gyms a day, you can theoretically earn battle hearts for 20 different buddies by swapping them between gym locations.
