<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Training Zones and Exercise Selection">

A critical component of **ultimate gym how to play** is understanding the three-zone system. The gym is physically divided into areas that specialize in different aspects of fitness. If you spend all your time in the wrong zone, your character's growth will not align with your specific goals.

### The Three Pillars of Training
The **Strength Zone** is designed for those who want to see the highest numbers possible on their Personal Records. Here, the weight increments are steeper, and the focus is on raw power. The **Hypertrophy Zone** is the aesthetic choice; it maximizes visible muscle growth, making your character look larger and more defined. Finally, the **Endurance Zone** allows for longer training sessions and improved performance in bodyweight movements.

| Zone Type | Primary Goal | Best Exercises | Progress Signal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strength** | Maximize PRs | Bench Press, Squat, Deadlift | Higher successful weight |
| **Hypertrophy** | Visual muscle size | Dumbbells, Cable Machines | Increased character scale |
| **Endurance** | Sustained effort | Pull-ups, Dips, Calisthenics | Longer set duration |

<Callout type="info" title="Exercise Variety">
There are over 31 exercises available in the game. While it is tempting to stick to one machine, rotating through different movements ensures balanced muscle development and prevents progression plateaus.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Nutrition, Supplements, and the Shaker System">

In **Ultimate Gym**, lifting is only half the battle. To truly excel, you must master the economy of cash and nutrition. Every rep you perform generates cash, which should be immediately reinvested into your diet. This is a core part of **ultimate gym how to play** effectively in 2026.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Earn Initial Cash">
    Perform basic sets on any machine or rack. Even low-weight sets generate income that you can use at the gym shop.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Purchase Basic Food">
    Buy items like chicken, rice, or protein bars. These provide temporary buffs that increase the effectiveness of every rep you perform.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Invest in Supplements">
    Once you have a steady income, buy supplements like Creatine or Pre-workout. These offer higher multipliers but cost more cash.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Use the Shaker System">
    Combine your food and supplements in a Shaker. This allows you to stack multiple buffs into a single consumable item, saving inventory space and maximizing session efficiency.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Buff Management">
Do not activate high-tier buffs if you only plan to play for five minutes. Buffs tick down in real-time, so ensure you have a dedicated 15-20 minute window to train before consuming your best Shaker mixes.
</Callout>

| Item Type | Priority | Spending Advice | Avoid |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cash** | Essential | Keep a reserve for buffs | Spending 100% on one item |
| **Food** | High | Use for every session | Training with an empty stomach |
| **Supplements** | Medium | Buy for PR attempts | Using them for light warm-ups |
| **Shaker** | High | Mix compatible buffs | Mixing conflicting stat items |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Advanced Progression: Bulk, Cut, and Calisthenics">

As you move past the beginner stage, **ultimate gym how to play** shifts toward body composition and advanced skill paths. The game allows you to choose between "Bulking" and "Cutting" phases, which significantly impact your character's appearance and lifting potential.

### The Bulk and Cut Cycle
Bulking increases your muscle mass and strength potential but may reduce your definition. Cutting, on the other hand, makes your muscles pop with definition and is often used before showing off your character or competing on leaderboards.

| Phase | Main Goal | Training Focus | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bulk** | Maximum Size | Strength/Hypertrophy | Large, powerful physique |
| **Cut** | High Definition | Endurance/Hypertrophy | Lean, shredded appearance |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Strategy">
Most top-tier players stay in a Bulk phase until they reach the "Master" rank, then perform a final Cut to achieve the "Grand Master" aesthetic. This ensures you have enough raw strength to hit the required PRs for higher ranks.
</Callout>
