<Section icon={Map} title="Optimizing Leg Training Zones: Strength vs Hypertrophy">

The gym layout is divided into three distinct training zones: Strength, Hypertrophy, and Endurance. Each zone modifies how your character gains attributes and interacts with different weights. For an optimal leg routine, you must strategically transition between these zones rather than sticking to a single area indefinitely.

The Strength zone is designed for low-repetition, high-weight lifting, which is essential for pushing your maximum lift capacity. Conversely, the Hypertrophy zone focuses on moderate weights with higher repetition volume, which is the primary driver for physical muscle growth and character size.

| Training Zone | Primary Focus | Stamina Cost | Growth Speed | Progress Signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Strength** | Max Weight / PRs | High | Moderate | Higher Lift Weight |
| **Hypertrophy** | Muscle Size | Moderate | Fast | Visible Muscle Mass |
| **Endurance** | Stamina / Reps | Low | Slow | Longer Workouts |

To maximize your leg development, use a hybrid training approach. Spend the majority of your early sessions in the Hypertrophy zone to build a solid foundation of muscle mass. Once your physical size increases, transition to the Strength zone to convert that mass into raw power and set new personal records.

| Training Phase | Zone Focus | Recommended Weight | Target Repetitions |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1: Foundation** | Hypertrophy | 70-80% of Max | 8 to 12 reps |
| **Phase 2: Peak Power** | Strength | 90-100% of Max | 1 to 5 reps |
| **Phase 3: Conditioning** | Endurance | 50-60% of Max | 15+ reps |

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Management">
Lifting near-maximum weights in the Strength zone drains your stamina rapidly. Avoid training to absolute failure on every set, as this reduces your overall training efficiency and limits your total XP gain per session.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step Lower Body Progression Path">

Transitioning from a gym beginner to a heavy-lifting veteran requires a systematic approach. Rushing into heavy weights too early will result in failed lifts and wasted training time. Follow this structured progression path to build your lower body strength efficiently and safely.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Your Baseline">

  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Build a Hypertrophy Foundation">
    Once you have unlocked basic equipment, transition to the Hypertrophy zone. Increase your weight gradually to around 75% of your maximum capacity and perform consistent sets to stimulate visible muscle growth and expand your leg size.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Reinvest Cash into Nutrition">
    Use the cash earned from your consistent training sessions to purchase high-quality food and supplements. Consuming these items before entering the training zone provides massive temporary buffs that accelerate your progression.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Test Your Maximum Strength">
    Move to the Strength zone and prepare for personal record attempts. Select weights close to your absolute limit, activate your supplement buffs, and perform low-rep sets to push your maximum leg strength to new heights.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Following this loop ensures that you are always training with purpose. Never lift without a clear goal; either focus on building size in the Hypertrophy zone or building raw power in the Strength zone.

<Callout type="info" title="Progression Speed">
